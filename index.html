I am a PhD candidate in Public Policy at The University of Chicago

‍

I am an applied microeconomist and my research revolve around the study of firms and schools responses to government regulations, and their effects on the sorting of students and workers into workplaces and schools. More broadly, my interests lay in labor economics, educational policy, and their inequality and efficiency consequences.

‍

I'm particularly interested in the analysis of incomplete markets, where allocations cannot be determined by market clearing prices (e.g. spots in school).

‍

If you're interested in talking about my work, or yours, get in touch!

You can reach me at descobarsalce@uchicago.edu
